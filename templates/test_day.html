IELTS Test Day: What to Expect

Be Prepared

Knowing what to expect on test day can help reduce stress and improve your performance. This guide will walk you through what happens from the moment you arrive at the test center until you leave.

Before Your Test Day

Essential Preparation

What to Bring

  • Original ID document - typically your passport (must be the same ID used when registering)
  • Test confirmation - your confirmation email or letter
  • Stationery - pencils, eraser, and sharpener (though these are usually provided)
  • Water - in a transparent bottle with no label

What NOT to Bring

  • Electronic devices - phones, smartwatches, tablets, etc.
  • Study materials - notes, books, dictionaries
  • Valuable items - jewelry or large amounts of money
  • Food - unless approved for medical reasons
  • Watches - typically not allowed in the test room

Important Notice

Personal items including electronic devices must be stored in designated areas. You are not allowed to access them during breaks. Any attempt to cheat can result in disqualification and may affect future test applications.

On Test Day

Arrival

Arrive at least 30 minutes before your test start time. This allows time for registration, security checks, and finding your way to the test room.

Late arrivals may not be admitted, resulting in forfeiture of your test fee.

Registration

You'll need to:

  • Show your ID for verification
  • Have your photograph taken (for security purposes)
  • Sign a registration form confirming your identity

Listening, Reading and Writing Tests

These three tests are always taken together in a single sitting with no breaks between them.

Order of tests:

  1. Listening: 30 minutes + 10 minutes transfer time
  2. Reading: 60 minutes
  3. Writing: 60 minutes

Total time: approximately 2 hours and 40 minutes

Speaking Test

The Speaking test may be scheduled on the same day as the other tests or up to 7 days before or after, depending on the test center.

You'll receive notification about the time and date of your Speaking test when you register, or shortly afterward.

Duration: 11-14 minutes

During the Test

Test Room Rules

  • Listen carefully to all instructions from the invigilator
  • Raise your hand if you need assistance
  • Do not speak to other test takers
  • If you need to use the bathroom, raise your hand - you'll be escorted
  • Stay seated until all materials are collected and you're dismissed

Test-Taking Tips

  • Keep track of time; there are no reminders except at the end
  • For Listening, read questions in advance to know what to listen for
  • For Reading, quickly scan the questions before reading the text
  • For Writing, spend a few minutes planning your response
  • For Speaking, relax and speak naturally; it's a conversation

After the Test

Results and Next Steps

Getting Your Results

  • Results are typically available 13 calendar days after your test
  • You can view your results online using your login details
  • A Test Report Form (TRF) will be sent to you by mail if you chose this option when registering
  • Results are valid for 2 years from the test date

If You Need to Retake

  • You can take the test as many times as you wish
  • Allow at least 3-4 weeks between tests for improved preparation
  • Analyze what went wrong in your previous attempt
  • Focus your preparation on your weakest areas
  • Consider getting professional feedback on your performance

Did You Know?

If you're unhappy with your result, you can request a remark (Enquiry on Results) within 6 weeks of your test date. A fee applies, which is refunded if your band score changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you arrive late, you may not be allowed to take the test, and you might forfeit your test fee. It's strongly recommended to arrive at least 30 minutes before your test is scheduled to begin.

If you know in advance that you can't make it to your test, visit the official IELTS website for information about test postponement or cancellation policies.

Policies vary by test center. Most centers provide pencils, erasers, and sharpeners. However, you're usually allowed to bring your own basic stationery. Avoid bringing pencil cases or containers; if you do, expect that they will be inspected.

Only standard pencils are allowed (no mechanical pencils or pens).

Most test centers do not allow watches of any kind in the test room. There will be a clock visible in the test room for you to keep track of time.

Smartwatches are strictly prohibited as they could be used for cheating.

If you need to use the bathroom during the test, raise your hand and wait for an invigilator to escort you. Note that:

  • The test clock will not stop while you're away from your desk
  • You may be subject to additional security screening upon returning
  • It's recommended to use the bathroom before the test begins

IELTS provides modified test materials and special arrangements for test takers with disabilities or special requirements. These might include:

  • Extra time
  • Special versions of test papers (braille, large print)
  • Special equipment (magnifiers, amplification devices)
  • Separate room

You must request special arrangements at least 3 months before your test date and provide supporting medical evidence.
