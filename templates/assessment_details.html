What to Expect

{% if assessment_type == 'listening' %}

This listening assessment consists of four recorded sections, with questions to answer as you listen. You will only hear each recording once, so pay close attention.

  • Section 1: A conversation between two people in an everyday social context
  • Section 2: A monologue in an everyday social context
  • Section 3: A conversation between up to four people in an academic context
  • Section 4: A monologue on an academic subject

Question types include multiple choice, matching, plan/map/diagram labeling, form/note/table/summary completion, and sentence completion.

{% elif assessment_type == 'reading' %}

This reading assessment consists of three passages with a total of 40 questions. The texts become increasingly difficult.

If you're taking Academic Reading, the passages will come from books, journals, magazines, and newspapers, written for a non-specialist audience. At least one text will contain a detailed argument.

If you're taking General Training Reading, the passages will come from notices, advertisements, company handbooks, official documents, books, magazines and newspapers.

Question types include multiple choice, identifying information, identifying writer's views/claims, matching information/headings/features/sentence endings, sentence completion, summary/note/table/flow-chart completion, and diagram label completion.

{% elif assessment_type == 'writing' %}

This writing assessment consists of two tasks. You must complete both tasks.

  • Task 1 (20 minutes): {% if 'Academic' in assessment.title %} You'll be presented with a graph, table, chart, or diagram and asked to describe, summarize, or explain the information in your own words. You may need to describe a process, describe an object or event, or explain how something works. {% else %} You'll be presented with a situation and asked to write a letter requesting information or explaining the situation. You may need to write to a friend or an official letter. {% endif %}
  • Task 2 (40 minutes): You'll be asked to write an essay in response to a point of view, argument, or problem. This task is worth twice as much as Task 1 in your final score.

Both tasks are assessed based on task achievement/response, coherence and cohesion, lexical resource, and grammatical range and accuracy.

{% elif assessment_type == 'speaking' %}

This speaking assessment consists of three parts and will assess your ability to communicate in English effectively.

  • Part 1 (4-5 minutes): Introduction and general questions about familiar topics (e.g., home, family, work, studies, interests)
  • Part 2 (3-4 minutes): You'll be given a card with a topic and points to include. You'll have 1 minute to prepare, then 2 minutes to speak about the topic.
  • Part 3 (4-5 minutes): Discussion of more abstract issues related to the topic in Part 2

Your speaking is assessed based on fluency and coherence, lexical resource, grammatical range and accuracy, and pronunciation.

{% endif %}

Tips for Success

{% if assessment_type == 'listening' %}

Read questions first

Look at the questions before listening so you know what to listen for.

Listen for signpost words

Words like "first," "however," "in contrast," help identify important information.

Check spellings

Spelling counts! Double-check your answers before submitting.

Don't leave blanks

There's no penalty for wrong answers, so always put something down.

{% elif assessment_type == 'reading' %}

Skim first, then read deeply

Quickly skim the passage to get the main idea before reading in detail.

Manage your time

Allocate about 20 minutes per passage to ensure you complete all three.

Look for keywords

Identify keywords in questions to help locate answers in the passage.

Watch for synonyms

The passage often uses synonyms of words found in the questions.

{% elif assessment_type == 'writing' %}

Plan your response

Spend 3-5 minutes planning your essay before you start writing.

Address all parts of the task

Make sure you fully address all aspects of the prompt.

Use paragraphs effectively

Organize your writing into clear paragraphs with one main idea each.

Proofread

Save 2-3 minutes at the end to check for errors.

{% elif assessment_type == 'speaking' %}

Speak naturally

Don't memorize answers. Speak naturally and fluently.

Use the preparation time

For Part 2, use your prep time to organize your thoughts.

Extend your answers

Aim to give detailed responses (but not excessively long ones).

Pronunciation matters

Work on clear pronunciation of sounds, word stress, and intonation.

{% endif %}
