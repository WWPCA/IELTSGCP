IELTS Life Skills

Purpose

IELTS Life Skills is specifically designed for UK visa applications in the 'family of a settled person' and 'settlement' categories. It assesses speaking and listening skills only.

Test Overview

IELTS Life Skills is different from other IELTS tests because:

  • It tests speaking and listening only
  • It is available at two levels: A1 and B1
  • You take the test with another candidate
  • The test is shorter (16-22 minutes)
  • It focuses on everyday communication skills
  • It has a pass/fail result rather than a band score
Note: IELTS Life Skills is a Secure English Language Test (SELT) approved by UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI).

Test Formats

IELTS Life Skills is available at two CEFR levels:

IELTS Life Skills - A1

Purpose: For those applying for a 'family of a settled person' visa.

Duration: 16-18 minutes

Skills Tested: Basic speaking and listening skills needed for everyday life.

At this level, you should be able to:

  • Understand and use basic phrases and expressions
  • Introduce yourself and ask/answer simple questions
  • Interact in a simple way if the other person speaks slowly and clearly
  • Understand simple instructions and directions

IELTS Life Skills - B1

Purpose: For those applying for indefinite leave to remain (settlement) or British citizenship.

Duration: 20-22 minutes

Skills Tested: Independent speaking and listening skills needed for everyday life.

At this level, you should be able to:

  • Express opinions and explain advantages/disadvantages
  • Start, maintain and close simple face-to-face conversations
  • Understand and extract specific information from speech
  • Communicate in familiar contexts and briefly give reasons and explanations

Test Format and Tasks

The IELTS Life Skills test has a practical focus and is conducted with two candidates and one examiner. It consists of three parts:

Part 1: Interview and Short Discussion

  • The examiner will ask you and the other candidate to introduce yourselves
  • You will answer simple questions about yourselves
  • You will have a short discussion on an everyday topic
  • For A1 level, the questions will be very simple
  • For B1 level, you'll need to express and justify opinions

Part 2: Collaborative Task

  • You will work together with the other candidate to complete a task
  • You might need to plan something together (like an outing)
  • You'll need to ask questions, make suggestions, and respond to the other candidate
  • For A1 level, the planning will be simple
  • For B1 level, you'll need to discuss advantages and disadvantages

Part 3: Discussion and Listening Activity

  • You will listen to a recording and answer questions
  • You will have a final discussion related to the recording
  • For A1 level, the listening task will be simple with concrete information
  • For B1 level, you'll need to extract specific details and discuss them
Unlike other IELTS tests:
  • Life Skills does not test reading or writing
  • You receive a "Pass" or "Fail" result rather than a band score
  • You interact with another candidate as well as the examiner

Which UK Visa Applications Require IELTS Life Skills?

Visa Type Required Level
Family of a settled person visa A1 Life Skills
Extension of stay as a partner or parent A2 Life Skills
Settlement (Indefinite Leave to Remain) B1 Life Skills
British Citizenship B1 Life Skills
Important: Always check the latest requirements on the official UK government website as they may change over time.

Sample Questions

A1 Level Sample Questions

Part 1: Personal Questions

  • What is your name?
  • Where do you live?
  • Do you work or study?
  • What do you like doing in your free time?

Part 2: Collaborative Task

"You and your partner want to meet for coffee. Decide together:

  • When you will meet
  • Where you will meet
  • How long you will stay"

B1 Level Sample Questions

Part 1: Discussion

  • What kind of public transport do you use most often?
  • Why do you prefer this type of transport?
  • How often do you use public transport?
  • What are the advantages of using public transport in your area?

Part 2: Collaborative Task

"You and your partner are planning a day trip to London. You need to decide:

  • What sights you want to see
  • How you will travel around the city
  • Where you will eat lunch
  • What time you will return home"

Preparing for IELTS Life Skills

To prepare for IELTS Life Skills, focus on improving these skills:

  • Listening carefully to what others say and responding appropriately
  • Taking turns in conversations and not dominating the discussion
  • Asking questions to clarify information or keep a conversation going
  • Making suggestions and responding to others' suggestions
  • Expressing opinions and giving reasons (especially for B1 level)
  • Understanding instructions and following them accurately
Tip: Practice speaking English with friends in everyday situations. Try planning activities together, discussing preferences, and extracting information from conversations or recordings.
