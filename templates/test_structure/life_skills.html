Test Format

Unlike the traditional IELTS test, IELTS Life Skills focuses only on speaking and listening skills. It's specifically designed for people who need to prove their English speaking and listening ability for UK visa purposes.

Key Information

  • Format: Face-to-face speaking and listening test with one examiner and two test takers
  • Three levels available: A1, A2, and B1 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)
  • Test duration:
    • A1 level: 16-18 minutes
    • A2 level: 20 minutes
    • B1 level: 22 minutes
  • Focus: Real-life communication skills in English

Which IELTS Life Skills Level Do I Need?

The level you need depends on your visa type:

  • IELTS Life Skills A1: For family of settled persons visa
  • IELTS Life Skills A2: For extending stay in the UK on a family visa
  • IELTS Life Skills B1: For settlement and British citizenship applications

Always check the specific requirements for your visa category on the UK government website, as these can change.

Sample Test Format (B1 Level)

Introduction: The examiner will ask you and the other test taker to introduce yourselves and confirm your identities.

Discussion: You'll have a short discussion about a familiar topic with the other test taker. The examiner will give you a prompt card with some suggested questions to ask each other.

Skills tested: Asking for and giving personal information, expressing opinions, taking turns in conversation.

Listening: You'll listen to a short recording (about 30 seconds to 1 minute) containing factual information. You'll be allowed to take notes.

Planning: You'll work together with the other test taker to plan something based on the information you heard. For example, you might need to plan an event or decide on the best option from several choices.

Skills tested: Listening for specific information, suggesting ideas, agreeing and disagreeing.

Discussion: You'll have a more in-depth discussion with the other test taker about a topic related to the theme of Part 2. The examiner will give you a prompt card with discussion points.

Skills tested: Expressing and justifying opinions, comparing and contrasting, turn-taking in conversation.

Assessment Criteria

The assessment is based on your ability to:

  • Obtain information from spoken English
  • Convey information using spoken English
  • Speak with others to share information, ideas, and opinions
  • Understand and engage in discussion with another person

The test is pass/fail based on the level you're taking (A1, A2, or B1). To pass, you must demonstrate that your speaking and listening skills meet the required level.

How to Book IELTS Life Skills

  1. Check the level of IELTS Life Skills you need for your visa application.
  2. Find an approved UKVI test center near you through the official IELTS website.
  3. Book your test online or through your local test center, specifying that you need IELTS Life Skills and the required level (A1, A2, or B1).
  4. Be prepared to provide biometric data (photograph and finger scan) on the test day.
  5. Bring your passport as identification on the test day.
