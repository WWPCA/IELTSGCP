IELTS for UKVI (UK Visas and Immigration)

Purpose

{{ test_info.description }}

Test Format

IELTS for UKVI has the same content, format, and level of difficulty as the standard IELTS test. The difference is that it's specifically approved for UK visa and immigration purposes and is conducted in UKVI-approved centers with increased security measures.

Key Information

  • Two versions available: You can take either IELTS Academic for UKVI or IELTS General Training for UKVI, depending on your visa requirements.
  • Special security: The test is conducted with enhanced security measures, including biometric testing (a photograph and finger scan).
  • Approved centers: The test must be taken at a location specially approved by UK Visas and Immigration.
  • Recognized globally: IELTS for UKVI is recognized not just by UK Visas and Immigration but also by universities, employers, and immigration authorities around the world.

Which Test Should I Take?

The test you need depends on your visa type:

  • Tier 1, 2, or 4 visas: Either Academic or General Training, depending on your specific circumstances
  • For study at degree level and above: Academic IELTS for UKVI
  • For work or study below degree level: General Training IELTS for UKVI
  • For family, settlement or ILR: Usually IELTS Life Skills (A1, A2 or B1 level)

Test Content

Listening

The Listening test for IELTS for UKVI follows the same format as the standard IELTS test:

  • Duration: 30 minutes, plus 10 minutes to transfer your answers
  • Sections: 4 recorded sections, with increasing difficulty
  • Questions: 40 questions in total
  • Format: Multiple choice, matching, plan/map/diagram labeling, form completion, note completion, table completion, flow-chart completion, summary completion, sentence completion, short-answer questions

Academic vs. General Training: The Listening component is identical for both Academic and General Training versions of IELTS for UKVI.

Reading

The Reading test format differs slightly between Academic and General Training versions of IELTS for UKVI:

Academic Reading

  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Texts: 3 long texts from books, journals, magazines, and newspapers
  • Topics: Academic, factual, analytical
  • Questions: 40 questions in total

General Training Reading

  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Sections: 3 sections with increasing difficulty
  • Topics: Everyday contexts, workplace situations, general interest
  • Questions: 40 questions in total

Question types for both versions: Multiple choice, identifying information, identifying writer's views/claims, matching information, matching headings, matching features, matching sentence endings, sentence completion, summary completion, note completion, table completion, flow-chart completion, diagram label completion, short-answer questions.

Writing

The Writing test also differs between Academic and General Training versions of IELTS for UKVI:

Academic Writing

  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Task 1: Describe visual information (graph, table, chart, diagram) in 150+ words
  • Task 2: Write an essay on a given topic in 250+ words
  • Focus: Academic language and analytical thinking

General Training Writing

  • Duration: 60 minutes
  • Task 1: Write a letter requesting information or explaining a situation in 150+ words
  • Task 2: Write an essay on a given topic in 250+ words
  • Focus: Everyday language and practical communication

Assessment: Both tasks are assessed on Task Achievement, Coherence and Cohesion, Lexical Resource, and Grammatical Range and Accuracy. Task 2 carries more weight than Task 1 in determining your final Writing band score.

Speaking

The Speaking test for IELTS for UKVI is identical for both Academic and General Training versions:

  • Duration: 11-14 minutes
  • Format: Face-to-face interview with a certified IELTS examiner
  • Recording: The test is recorded for security purposes

The test consists of three parts:

  1. Part 1: Introduction and Interview (4-5 minutes)

    The examiner introduces themselves and asks you general questions about familiar topics such as home, family, work, studies, and interests.

  2. Part 2: Long Turn (3-4 minutes)

    You're given a task card with a topic and points to cover. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes on the topic. The examiner may ask one or two questions when you finish.

  3. Part 3: Discussion (4-5 minutes)

    The examiner asks further questions connected to the topic in Part 2, allowing for a more in-depth discussion of abstract ideas and issues.

UKVI Special Requirement: For IELTS for UKVI, you'll need to provide biometric data (photograph and finger scan) before taking the Speaking test, as part of the enhanced security procedures.

How to Book IELTS for UKVI

  1. Check which version of the test you need for your visa application.
  2. Visit the official IELTS website at ielts.org/take-a-test/booking-your-test to find an approved UKVI test center near you.
  3. Book your test online, specifying that you need IELTS for UKVI.
  4. Be prepared to provide biometric data (photograph and finger scan) on the test day.
  5. Bring your passport as identification on the test day.
