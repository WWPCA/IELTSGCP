IELTS Online Test

Purpose

IELTS Online allows you to take the IELTS Academic test from the comfort of your home or another suitable location, monitored by a remote proctor.

What is IELTS Online?

IELTS Online is a computer-delivered version of the IELTS Academic test that can be taken from your home or another suitable location. The test is monitored by a remote proctor via your webcam, microphone, and screen sharing to maintain test security and integrity.

Important: Currently, only the Academic version of IELTS is available online. The General Training version is not available in the online format.
Note: IELTS Online is not accepted for all purposes. It is generally not accepted for immigration purposes, including UK, Australian, Canadian or New Zealand visas. Always check with your receiving organization whether they accept IELTS Online before booking.

Test Format

The IELTS Online test has the same format, content, timing, and scoring as the in-person IELTS Academic test:

Section Duration Description
Listening 30 minutes + 10 minutes transfer time 4 recordings, 40 questions
Reading 60 minutes 3 academic texts, 40 questions
Writing 60 minutes Task 1: Describe visual information (150 words)
Task 2: Essay (250 words)
Speaking 11-14 minutes Video call interview with an IELTS examiner
Note: All four test components (Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking) may be completed on the same day or the Speaking test may be scheduled up to 3 days before or after the other components.

Technical Requirements

To take the IELTS Online test, you must have:

Computer Requirements

  • Windows 10 or 11, or macOS 10.15 or higher
  • Minimum 4GB RAM
  • Minimum 1 GB available disk space
  • Screen resolution of at least 1280 x 720
  • Administrator rights to install the secure browser

Internet Requirements

  • Stable internet connection
  • Minimum bandwidth of 1 Mbps upload and download
  • Wired connection recommended (more stable than Wi-Fi)
  • No VPN, proxy or virtual machines

Webcam & Microphone

  • Working webcam (internal or external)
  • Working microphone and speakers/headphones
  • Ability to position the webcam to show your face and shoulders clearly

ID Requirements

  • Valid government-issued photo ID
  • Must match the ID details provided during registration
  • Must be the original document (not a photocopy)
Important: You'll need to complete a system check before booking to ensure your computer meets all requirements.

Test Environment Requirements

Your test environment must meet these requirements:

  • Quiet, private room where you won't be disturbed
  • Clear desk and area around your computer (no books, papers, notes)
  • Good lighting so the proctor can see you clearly
  • No other people in the room during the test
  • No phones or electronic devices (other than your test computer)
  • No watches (smart or analog)
  • One clear water bottle allowed (no other food or drink)
Room scan: Before starting the test, you'll be asked to use your webcam to show the proctor around your room and testing area.

Test Day Process

Before the Test

  • Set up your computer in a suitable location
  • Close all applications except the secure browser
  • Have your ID ready
  • Log in 15-30 minutes before your scheduled time

Check-in Process

  • Connect with the online proctor
  • Verify your identity using your ID
  • Complete a room scan using your webcam
  • Receive test instructions from the proctor

During the Test

  • The proctor will monitor you via webcam and screen sharing
  • Complete the Listening, Reading, and Writing sections
  • You can communicate with the proctor using the chat feature if needed
  • Short breaks are allowed between test sections (but you must stay at your desk)

Speaking Test

  • Connect with an IELTS examiner via video call
  • Complete the three parts of the Speaking test
  • The format is identical to the in-person Speaking test

After the Test

  • Results available in 3-6 days
  • Access your results online
  • Electronic Test Report Form (TRF) available
  • Paper TRF can be sent to you by mail if requested

Advantages and Limitations

Advantages

  • Take the test from your home
  • No need to travel to a test center
  • More test dates and times available
  • Results available in 3-6 days
  • Familiar environment may reduce test anxiety
  • More flexible scheduling options

Limitations

  • Not accepted for immigration purposes
  • Only Academic format available (no General Training)
  • Strict technical requirements
  • Need a suitable test environment
  • Risk of technical issues affecting the test
  • Not available in all countries

Is IELTS Online Right for You?

Consider taking IELTS Online if:

  • You need an IELTS Academic test for study or professional registration (not immigration)
  • You have confirmed your receiving organization accepts IELTS Online
  • You have a suitable computer and testing environment
  • You are comfortable with technology
  • You prefer the convenience of testing from home

Consider taking an in-person IELTS test if:

  • You need the test for immigration purposes
  • You need the General Training format
  • You don't have the required technical setup
  • You don't have a suitable quiet room for testing
  • You prefer a controlled test center environment
Always check: Before booking IELTS Online, always verify with your receiving organization (university, professional body, etc.) that they accept IELTS Online results.
